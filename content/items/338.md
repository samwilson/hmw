## Transcription

Aus 406376 P/O W.M. Wilson\
75 OPERATIONAL TRAINING UNIT\
R.A.F.\
MIDDLE EAST FORCES.

Senders Address: 131 South Terrace\
South Perth\
West Aust.\
Date: 15th August 1943

Dear Murray,

Margaret has asked me to write
you this as she is looking after my children at
the moment and there is no post office handy
to our home. She has been very generous — Alan
is just having his first holidays since we came
south, and she and we have exchanged
homes for the weekend. It is very pleasant for us,
and just the sort of thing Marg would think
of. Joan's engagement was in Saturday's
paper. She was terribly lucky — was sent to
Adelaide unexpectedly over a week end on escort
duty and was able to see Geoff. You'd hardly
know Judith these days — she has grown so, and is
improved in all ways since her operation. Marg
looks well and very pretty — has a new perm
and a new grey and white dress which suits
her very well. She always seems busy, one way and
another — she remarked that she must do all her
"good deeds" now, as when you come home she is
going to be completely unavailable to anybody else.
Oh Murray, it is my dearest wish that that can
be very soon. Hope you're fit and well.

Very affectionately\
*C Berryman*

(CONSTANCE BERRYMAN)
