## Transcription

{u|Copy of Letter No.6 from Sgt. Pilot Murray Wilson.}

Halifax,\
22nd. August, 1941.

We've been strutting around on dry land for a few days now and have we enjoyed
it? We've seen most of what there is to see about Halifax, but its
not much of a dump and I for one won't be sorry to get away but any town
anywhere, would have seemed decent to us after our spell at sea. We've
been around the shops and bought up a few things that we needed - most of
the boys found that they had forgotten something, and we've been all around
the town and nearby spots and now consider that we know quite a bit about
the local scenery.

Nova Scotia is officially dry - or nearly so. All beer etc,, is sold by the
Liquor Commission and it can only be consumed at home - if you've got one.
