MR. F. H. V. HESTER.

Roebourne, January 2.
Gloom was cast over the town yesterday,
owing to the fact that Mr. F. H. V.

rather suddenly in the hospital. Death
was due to an affection of the heart. The

{{{quotation
DEATH OF MR. F. H. V. HESTER

General regret was expressed on Monday
last when it became known that Mr. F. H.
V. Hester had succumbed to the affection
of the heart from which be suffered. A
few weeks previous to his death Mr. Hester
had been forced to seek medical advice,
and remained in town for a few days until
he felt sufficiently well to undertake the
trip back to the Turner River. Evidently
this trip was too trying for a man in his
weak state, however, and within a brief
period of his reaching his home, he had to
return to Roebourne, arriving here on
Sunday. His condition was at once regarded
as serious by Dr. Maunsell, though
the end came rather sooner than was expected.
Mr. Hester was an old identity of
tho Nor'-West, and highly-respected by
everyone with whom lie came in contact.
He had for many years been engaged in
pastoral pursuits, and a few years ago was
interested in commercial ventures at
Mallina and Balla Bulla, He was a justice
of the peace for the district, and at one
time a member of the Roebourne Roads
Board, displaying in both capacities strong
common sense, if not brilliancy. He succeeded
in wending his way along life's
rugged path without making any enemies,
whilst his friends embraced scores, and a
man who in these days of strife can show a
record like Mr. Hester's may indeed be
proud. The deceased, who was 44 years of
age, leaves a wife and one child to mourn
their loss. The funeral took place at the
Anglican cemetery on Tuesday morning,
the Rev. H. Pitts conducting the burial
service.
